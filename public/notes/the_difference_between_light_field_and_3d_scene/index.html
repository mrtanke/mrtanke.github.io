The difference between Light field and 3D scene

Paper-reading notes: The difference between Light field and 3D scene

For a 3D scene#

3D scene means every point has (x, y, z).

  • x = left–right
  • y = up–down
  • z = depth (camera)

For a 4D light field#

How the camera sees the world from many tiny angles. (A grid of 2D images, each slightly shifted because of parallax.)

$$ L(u, v, x, y) $$

Where:

  • $(x, y)$ = the spatial position on the image sensor.
  • $(u, v)$ = the position of the tiny camera in the camera array (e.g., 3×3 sub-aperture images).

E.g. a 3×3 camera array:

Cameras: (u,v): (0,0) (0,1) (0,2) (1,0) (1,1) (1,2) (2,0) (2,1) (2,2)

  • u = horizontal camera index
  • v = vertical camera index

Each camera produces its own 2D image (x,y).

(u, v) → choose which camera (angular dimension) (x, y) → choose which pixel inside that camera

The LF representation can mainly classified into three types including:

  • macro-pixel images (MacPIs),
  • sub-aperture images (SAIs),
  • epipolar-plane images (EPIs),

The images captured by a plenoptic camera like Lytro and Raytrix are referred to as MacPIs, which can be dismantled horizontally and vertically to obtain SAIs. Slicing the SAIs along its horizontal and vertical directions results in an EPI.
